Stablecoins fiat-collateralized

They are known as fiat-collateralized stablecoins because these stablecoins are backed by money issued by governments.

Example : It includes currencies like the USD, EUR, or GBP. As long as the economy of the currencyâ€™s country remains stable, so will fiat-backed stablecoins.

Fiat-backed stablecoins are collateralized 1:1.

That means for every stablecoin in circulation, a single unit of currency ($1) is kept in a bank account to back it up.

The stablecoins corresponding to the amount you retrieved will be taken out of circulation if you redeem them with the issuer.
