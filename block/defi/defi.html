What is Defi?

There are many different ways we use our money in the course of our life. Here some examples :

  • buying goods
  • exchanging our local currency for foreign ones
  • getting a loan
  • investing in a company
All these transactions are facilitated by financial corporations.
A typical financial corporations is a bank.
They are controlled and owned by a small group of shareholders.

Centralized Finance

Since anything we do with our money is controlled by these institutions, this system is known as centralized finance (CeFi) or traditional finance (TradFi).
This is the financial system we are all familiar with, in which third parties control all our transactions while charging a fee for their services.
For example, whenever you buy a cup of coffee by card, you and the merchant are not the only parties involved in the transaction.

Decentralized Finance

Decentralized finance (DeFi) offers us all the same financial instruments and opportunities of traditional finance :

  • buying goods
  • lending money for a yield
without any intermediary like banks or brokerage firms as they are replaced by blockchain and smart contracts.
Using DeFi applications, you will be able to
  • lend or borrow crypto from peers
  • trade crypto assets without any centralized entity
  • earn high interest
